The Innovation

The Scalpel,
not the Sledgehammer

Valor's breakthrough is e-LLE™ — Electrochemical Liquid-Liquid Extraction — a platform that uses "smart" molecules and low-voltage electricity to surgically extract specific metal ions from complex feeds.

The Western world faces a critical processing bottleneck. Incumbent technologies — pyrometallurgy and hydrometallurgy — rely on extreme heat (1,200°C) or massive chemical inputs. They are impossible to permit in the US, too expensive to build, and incapable of handling modern feedstock complexity.

e-LLE™ changes everything. Developed with funding from DOE's ARPA-E, DARPA, and the NSF, and protected by 10+ foundational patents, it is the only platform technology built for the scale, versatility, and economics that American reindustrialization demands.

Valor Launches to Reshore and Revolutionize Critical Minerals Processing in America

Ex-Glencore Executive and UIUC Scientists Unveil the Future of Critical Minerals Separations and Metals Production

Valor, a deep-tech startup, officially announced its launch with a bold mission: to rebuild the Western world's capacity to process and refine critical minerals. Co-founded by Kunal Sinha, Prof. Xiao Su, and Dr. Johannes Elbert of UIUC, Valor is commercializing e-LLE™ — a platform technology poised to disrupt traditional smelting-refining and hydrometallurgy.

Alongside its launch, the company announced four major milestones:

  • Oversubscribed First Funding Round: Co-led by Transition and Zero Infinity Partners (ZIP), and a powerhouse syndicate of industry angel investors.

  • Strategic Demonstration Site: Signed an agreement with Solugen to host Valor's demonstration-scale plant at their BioForge 1 site in Houston, Texas.

Reuters

US startup Valor targets domestic critical minerals processing with electrochemical technology

March 11, 2026

A US deep-tech startup launched Wednesday with a mission to reshore processing of minerals critical to the country's electrification and defense needs, using an electrochemical technology that its founders say is faster, cheaper, and more environmentally friendly than existing methods. Valor, headquartered in New York, said it has raised a seed funding round co-led by Transition and Zero Infinity Partners, and is commercializing a platform called e-LLE™ — Electrochemical Liquid-Liquid Extraction — developed at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. The company said it has secured exclusive global rights to the underlying IP and is targeting a demonstration plant in Houston.

The Wall Street Journal

Ex-Glencore Executive Launches Startup to Revive American Metals Refining

March 11, 2026

Kunal Sinha spent 14 years at commodity giant Glencore watching the West cede its metals-refining capacity to China. Now he is betting a breakthrough technology from a University of Illinois lab can change that. Valor, a startup he co-founded with two UIUC scientists, emerged from stealth Wednesday with backing from some of the mining and energy industry's most prominent names. Its proprietary process — Electrochemical Liquid-Liquid Extraction — uses custom-designed molecules and low-voltage electricity to separate metals with a precision that traditional smelting and chemical refining cannot match, the company says. The approach lets a single plant recover dozens of different metals from a single feed — a potentially transformative advantage in an industry built around single-metal facilities.
